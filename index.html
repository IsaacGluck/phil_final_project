Philosophy of Time and Time Travel
Use the Minkowski Spacetime Interval to calculate spacio-temporal distance.
Model Instructions Spacetime Interval $$I(p,q) = -\Delta t^2_A(p,q) + \Delta x^2_A(p,q)$$ $$\Delta t_A = {\sqrt{-I(p,q)}} \text{ if } \Delta x^2_A = 0$$ Instructions
  1. Press the Start button to begin the simulation.
  2. Yellow lines are the light cone at a point.
  3. Staying within the light cone, draw world lines by clicking and dragging the mouse.
    • This model makes assumptions regarding the arrow of time.
    • If a line does not stick, it is not possible.
    • Reasons include being outside the light cone or representing non-Einsteinian time travel.
  4. Press the Finish button to complete your path.
  5. Check out the righthand panel to see the stats regarding the time traveled over the worldline.
  6. Click on individual worldline segments to see stats regarding the time traveled on that segment.
  7. Click the Clear button to start over.
